How the format & points work

Every 9 holes is its own match. Front 9: your fixed pairing plays a 4BBB (better-ball) match against the opposite team's pairing, worth 2 team points. Concurrently, two 9-hole singles run inside that same four-ball — 1 point personal + 0.5 team point per singles win.

Back 9 rebrackets: if both front-9 four-balls finished decisively, winning pairs meet for the daily title, losing pairs meet for the wooden spoon. If either front-9 four-ball is halved, both groups simply replay the same pairing on the back 9 — no rebracket that day.

Each teammate is partnered with you exactly once across the 3 courses (round-robin pairing) — every combination happens once, nobody repeats.

Handicap allowances used here

You (Mark) play off scratch — everyone else's shots are the gap to your index, scaled by that course's Slope Rating, at a 90% allowance. This is now used for both four-ball and singles, so it's one number per player per course. Shots are taken on that 9's hardest-ranked holes (the stroke index on the card).

Daily Champions lose 1 shot, Daily Wooden Spoon gains 1 shot — applied from the next course onward once that day's back-9 rebracket is decided. These tables show the pre-trip baseline before any of that kicks in; the live match cards below adjust automatically as results come in.
